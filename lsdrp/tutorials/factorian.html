The Multitool

The multitool will be your main tool for mining before you research Drills and for constructing your setup once you've gotten all the appropriate buildings. It's for more than just construction, however, as it's a fundamental part of early stage automation.

Mining

Mining with the multitool is done by simply aiming at the largest part of an ore and holding left click. Each level milestone for Factorian increases ore yield via this method by 1.

Construction

The Multitool can be used to place buildings. Left click on a building to select it, then left click again to place it. R to rotate, right click to deselect. On flat ground, building placements snap to a grid. On uneven ground, they don't.

You can press R on already placed buildings to rotate them, or right click to collapse them into a pocketable item.

To link one building to another (for example, a drill to a storage), place the building with its arrow pointed towards the other building (this indicates where items are being sent to). Note that on a grid, buildings can only be linked if they're placed cardinal to each other, not diagonal. This limitation can be circumvented by placing the machine using the physgun and rotating it into place (to link it organically), or by linking it normally and then moving it (which doesn't break the link until it's moved too far for some reason).

Buildings

A brief overview of how the essential buildings work.

Drills

Drills must be placed close to an ore source. You can see a Drill's radius with the Multitool. When placed with the multitool or turned off and on again, it will begin mining whatever resource is in range. When linked to storage that can accept ores, it will send the ores there. Drills will occasionally find artifacts buried underground, which can be shot into space later for money and upgrades.

Melters

Melters require ore and coal in order to perform their function. Simply feed one both to begin the process.

Assemblers & Refiners

Functionally identical, except they craft different items from each other. To use them, select the item you want it to craft, then feed it the required materials, either by hand or by some form of automation. Typically, Assemblers are unable to construct large buildings.

Item Cannons

When linked to a container or a machine, spits items from that container or machine to another valid container of your choosing within a certain radius.

Bot Hives

Bot hives are largely a set-and-forget building. Place in the spot where its radius covers the most buildings and it'll do its job. For the best results, set the bot type to "gunship".

To put it simply, bots from the Bot Hive deliver items from one place to another as needed, effectively an advanced form of automation. With a high enough bot count and a large enough radius, you won't need any other method (except maybe linking for storage reasons).

The Rocket Silo

This is where you launch rockets into space to turn a profit. It requires a booster and a payload before it can launch. The payload type increases the number of item stacks you can send at a time. Every 10 minutes, there may be a deal going on where some locations will buy certain products at a higher price than normal. These are the best times to sell.

Words of Caution

Never leave your rocket unattended.

Not everything that comes off of it can be stolen, but any cash and logistic bots sent back absolutely can be.

Inventory your bulk items.

Items in the workbench can only stack up to 500. If you mass produce anything, it's better to put it in your inventory in one big stack instead of packing up carelessly, as you can lose a LOT of items and buildings doing that. The best things to do it with are ores, raw materials, and wires, since you may make too many of these at any given time.
